Core Programme Tracks

Choose the Skill Pathway That Moves the Learner Forward

NextPrep focuses on high-value skill areas with practical outputs. Every learner also receives foundational exposure to soft skills, entrepreneurial thinking, pitching, and business model development, regardless of their technical focus.

01

Digital Skills (Programming)

Learners understand how websites and apps are built, master HTML, CSS, and JavaScript basics, build mini-games and simple applications, solve real-world problems through code, and collaborate in mini-hackathons.

02

Data Skills (Analytics)

Learners understand data types, structure, and sources; clean data; analyse with Excel and Google Sheets; visualise data using charts and dashboards; and present insights with confidence.

03

Cybersecurity (Ethical Hacking)

Learners build cyber awareness, understand online threats, practise safe defensive thinking, explore ethical hacking concepts in controlled settings, and learn how to protect people, devices, and organisations.

04

Green Economy & Sustainability

Learners explore climate-smart thinking, sustainability challenges, circular economy ideas, social impact, green innovation, and how data and digital tools can support better environmental decisions.

05

AI Engineering

Learners move beyond casual AI use into structured AI thinking: prompts, workflows, automation concepts, responsible AI, AI-assisted products, and practical prototypes that solve real problems.

Plus

Career, Business & Presentation Skills

Every technical track includes communication, collaboration, pitching, entrepreneurial thinking, business model development, and confidence-building so learners can explain and apply what they build.

Hands-On Modules

What Learners Actually Build and Practise

The strongest training does not end with notes. It ends with evidence: working pages, dashboards, mini-projects, clear presentations, and better decision-making.

Digital Skills

Programming Foundations

For learners who need to understand how websites, apps, and interactive digital products are built.

Learn how websites and apps are built Master HTML, CSS, and JavaScript basics Build mini-games, animations, and simple applications Solve real-world problems through code Collaborate and showcase work in mini-hackathons
Hands-on modules include:
  • Web Foundations: HTML and CSS for creating clean pages.
  • Interactive Programming: JavaScript and logic building.
  • App Studio: Build a first web-based app.
  • Game Lab: Simple browser games with animations.
  • Mini Hackathons: Team coding challenges and presentations.
  • Tech for Good: Use coding to solve school or community problems.
Data Skills

Analytics and Dashboards

For learners who need to turn raw information into useful insight, charts, stories, and decisions.

Understand data types, structure, and sources Clean data using practical spreadsheet methods Analyse data using Excel and Google Sheets Visualise data using charts and dashboards Build storytelling, reasoning, and presentation skills with data
Hands-on modules include:
  • Spreadsheet Mastery: formulas, functions, formatting, and structured analysis.
  • Data Cleaning: focused analytics challenges using messy datasets.
  • Charts & Dashboards: visual reports and full capstone dashboards.
  • Intro to Analysis: mini-projects that turn questions into evidence.
  • Data for Social Impact: analyse real datasets and present practical recommendations.
Cybersecurity

Ethical Hacking and Digital Safety

For learners who need to understand risk, protect systems, and think responsibly about cyber threats.

Hands-on modules include:
  • Cyber Hygiene: passwords, devices, privacy, phishing, and everyday protection.
  • Threat Thinking: how attackers think and how defenders respond.
  • Safe Lab Practice: ethical hacking concepts in controlled learning environments.
  • Web Security Basics: common risks in websites and online accounts.
  • Incident Response: what to do when something goes wrong.
Green Economy

Sustainability and Social Impact

For learners who want to connect innovation, environmental responsibility, data, and practical community impact.

Hands-on modules include:
  • Climate and Sustainability Literacy: key ideas behind the green economy.
  • Circular Economy Thinking: waste, reuse, redesign, and smarter consumption.
  • Green Innovation Lab: identify local sustainability problems and solutions.
  • Sustainability Data: use data to understand environmental and social problems.
  • Impact Pitch: present a simple sustainability project with evidence.
AI Engineering

AI Workflows and Practical Prototypes

For learners who need to move from using AI casually to designing structured, useful AI-supported solutions.

Hands-on modules include:
  • AI Foundations: what AI can and cannot do, with responsible use principles.
  • Prompt Engineering: better instructions, better outputs, better judgement.
  • AI Workflow Design: research, writing, analysis, planning, and productivity systems.
  • Automation Concepts: connect tasks, documents, and data into repeatable workflows.
  • Prototype Studio: design an AI-assisted solution for a real problem.

Learning Model

How NextPrep Turns Training Into Capability

We keep learning structured and evidence-led. Learners do not just consume information; they practise, build, receive feedback, and present work that proves progress.

01

Diagnose the skill gap

We clarify the audience, current skill level, desired outcome, timeframe, and what proof of learning should look like.

02

Train with real use cases

Learners work with practical scenarios, tools, templates, datasets, documents, and project tasks that mirror real work.

03

Build visible outputs

Each programme pushes learners toward a finished output: a dashboard, pitch, portfolio piece, project plan, presentation, workflow, or prototype.

04

Present and improve

We review clarity, execution, confidence, and real-world usefulness so learning transfers into work, school, business, or opportunity.
